GETTING STARTED:

F1 for Help anywhere in BookTrakker.

Play with BookTrakker. Setting the data source for data lookup is controlled in Tools > Options > ISBN as well as in GrabIt, by clicking the plugins button at the top of Inventory, then the Settings tab, left side.

Do a New Record, click OK, click on the Keywords tab, then click into the ISBN field on the left. You are now in the ISBN field. Type in an ISBN, press Enter. If the ISBN is on Amazon and you are an Amazon Pro Merchant seller and you did the Amazon Seller Account Registration in App Manager then data is downloaded into BookTrakker. Finish data entry, then click Done or Next record to do another. ASIN works like ISBN if you get the ASIN from Amazon.
We also have ISBN ONLY lookup available from ISBNdb.com. You pay them $9.95/mo for their service. Create an account via Tools > Options > ISBN.

If you want your own Tab Order then do Tools > Tab Order, click F1 for Help. (See above if Help does not work)

The Sample Database has 100 records so you can see how data looks or to do some Hit Lists. do not allow it to exceed 120 records before doing Tools > Options > Records and clicking the Delete Sample Database button if you need to import data or start with fresh data entry, no data to import. (If we do the import then the database we send you replaces the Sample database)

Enter or Tab moves you through the data entry form.

Tools > Options is where we store defaults. Lots of them.

Uploads happen with the click of a button to all sites BookTrakker supports. OneClick.

BookTrakker has Hit Lists. Hit Lists allow you to find records based on Search Criteria, such as how many books are priced below $15 or £15 that were listed before 12/31/2007. Or just about any other Criteria you want. For Homebase users who had Catalogs, and imported their data, Catalogs are now Categories in Hit Lists: Category List. 

Once you understand how BookTrakker works you can either start entering records or import your data or download/install the database we created when we imported your data from Homebase or some other program.
